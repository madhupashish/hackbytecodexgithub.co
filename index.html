LINUX

Linux is a free and open-source operating system that was created in the early 1990s by Linus Torvalds. It was inspired by Unix and follows many of the same principles and design concepts. Linux is part of the Unix-like family of operating systems, often referred to as Unix-like or Unix-compatible.

80
UNIX

Unix is one of the oldest operating systems, dating back to the late 1960s. It was originally developed at Bell Labs by Ken Thompson, Dennis Ritchie, and others. Unix was designed to be a multi-user, multi-tasking system, allowing multiple users to access and run programs simultaneously.
